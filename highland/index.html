Thorough Termite Inspections

The first crucial step in effective termite control is a detailed and accurate inspection. Infestation Identification

Based on our findings, we accurately identify the type of termites present (e.g., subterranean, drywood, dampwood) and the extent of the infestation. This is crucial for determining the most appropriate and effective treatment method.

Damage Assessment

We assess any visible damage caused by termites and provide a clear explanation of our findings. While we are not structural engineers, we can point out areas that require attention and potentially recommend further evaluation by a qualified professional.

Risk Factor Analysis

We identify factors on your property that may contribute to termite infestations, such as moisture problems, wood-to-soil contact, or structural vulnerabilities. Addressing these risk factors is key to long-term prevention.

Targeted Termite Treatments

Based on the inspection findings, we develop a customized treatment plan to effectively eliminate the termite infestation and protect your property from future attacks. Our treatment options are safe, effective, and designed to minimize disruption to your daily life.

Liquid Termite Barriers

For subterranean termites, we often utilize liquid termiticides applied to the soil around the foundation of your property. This creates a protective barrier that termites cannot penetrate or that eliminates them upon contact. We use advanced products that are highly effective and designed for long-lasting control.

Termite Baiting Systems

Termite baiting systems involve placing bait stations around the perimeter of your property. Foraging termites discover the bait, consume it, and share it with the colony, ultimately leading to the colony's elimination. These systems are an environmentally conscious and effective option for many situations.

For drywood termites or localized infestations, we may apply direct wood treatments to affected areas. These treatments penetrate the wood, eliminating termites within the structure. We use fumigation treatments for more widespread drywood infestations if necessary.

Fumigation Services

In cases of extensive drywood termite infestations that are spread throughout the structure, fumigation may be the most effective solution. This involves enclosing the building and introducing a fumigant gas that penetrates all wood within the structure, eliminating termites. Serious Protection Strategies: Recognizing the Signs You Need Termite Control in Highland, CA

Termites can be incredibly elusive, often causing significant damage before their presence is even suspected. However, being aware of the common signs of a termite infestation can help you catch the problem early and minimize the potential for costly repairs. Unfortunately, many homeowners in Highland and across California are unaware of these warning signs, allowing termite colonies to grow and inflict increasing damage over time. Recognizing these indicators is the first critical step in protecting your valuable property from these tireless pests. Don't wait until the damage is extensive; proactive identification is key to effective termite control in Highland, CA.

Discarded Termite Wings

One of the most visible signs of a termite infestation, particularly drywood termites, is the presence of discarded wings. After a swarming event, where reproductive termites (swarmers) leave the colony to establish new ones, they shed their wings. You might find piles of these translucent wings near windowsills, doorways, or light sources, indicating that a termite swarm has recently occurred in or around your property. Finding these wings, even in small quantities, is a strong indicator that a termite colony is nearby and potentially inside your structure. Swift action in contacting a professional for termite control in Highland, CA, is essential upon discovering discarded wings.

Mud Tubes on Walls or Foundations

Subterranean termites, which are very common in California, require moisture to survive and travel between their underground colonies and their food source (your wooden structures). They build protective mud tubes, often the width of a pencil, along walls, foundations, and other surfaces to shield themselves as they travel back and forth. These tubes are made of soil, wood, and termite saliva and serve as highways for the termites. Seeing these mud tubes branching out from the ground up towards your property is a clear sign of subterranean termite activity and an urgent need for professional termite control in Highland, CA. Disturbing these tubes will not eliminate the colony; it will simply cause the termites to build new ones or find alternative routes.

Hollow-Sounding Wood

Termites eat wood from the inside out, leaving a thin veneer of wood or paint on the surface. If you tap on a wooden structure suspected of being infested and it sounds hollow, it's a strong indicator of internal termite damage. You can use a screwdriver handle to tap on baseboards, door frames, or other wooden elements. A solid thud indicates healthy wood, while a hollow sound suggests that termites have been at work within. Discovering hollow-sounding wood during a routine inspection or due to noticeable structural flex is a serious indication that professional termite control in Highland, CA is required immediately to prevent catastrophic damage.

Termite Droppings (Frass)

Drywood termites create small, pellet-like droppings known as frass. These droppings are often pushed out of tiny 'kick-out' holes in the wood near where the termites are actively feeding. The color of the frass can vary depending on the type of wood the termites are consuming. Finding small piles of what looks like sawdust or coarse sand, particularly near wooden furniture, window frames, or baseboards, is a common sign of a drywood termite infestation. These piles are often found directly below the kick-out holes. The presence of frass is a definitive indicator of a drywood termite issue requiring specialized termite control in Highland, CA.

Bubbling or "Bubbled" Paint or Wallpaper

As termites consume wood beneath paint or wallpaper, they can cause these surface materials to blister, bubble, or peel. This is often mistaken for water damage, but it can be a sign of termite activity. The presence of these anomalies without a clear source of moisture should raise suspicion and warrant further investigation for potential termite infestation. Termites can also create tunnels and galleries just beneath the surface, which can manifest as faint lines or ridges visible through paint or wallpaper. These subtle signs can be easily overlooked but are important indicators that professional termite control in Highland, CA is necessary to confirm or deny the presence of termites.

Sticking Doors or Windows

Termite infestations can cause wooden door and window frames to warp and swell as the termites consume the wood and create moisture. This can lead to doors and windows becoming difficult to open or close, sticking in their frames. While sticking doors and windows can have other causes, such as humidity changes, if combined with other potential termite signs, it should be investigated as a possible indicator of an infestation. The subtle changes in the structural integrity of the wood caused by termite tunneling can often lead to these issues. If you experience persistent sticking of doors or windows that cannot be attributed to other factors, contacting a professional for termite control in Highland, CA is a prudent step.

Visible Termite Swarmers

Seeing winged termites, also known as swarmers, emerging from your home or property, particularly during warmer months, is a definitive sign of an active termite infestation. Swarming is how termites reproduce and establish new colonies. While seeing a swarm is alarming, it confirms the presence of a mature termite colony nearby. Swarms are often attracted to light and may be observed near windows, doors, or exterior lights. Witnessing a termite swarm requires immediate action, as it indicates that a significant infestation is likely present within the structure. Contacting San Bernardino Termite for expert termite control in Highland, CA, is the most effective way to address this issue and prevent further colony development.
